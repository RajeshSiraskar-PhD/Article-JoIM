\section*{Literature Survey: AutoRL for Predictive Maintenance in Manufacturing (2020--2026)}
\begin{spacing}{1.05}

\textbf{Audience:} Industrial practitioners (maintenance leaders, reliability engineers, digital manufacturing teams).

\textbf{Scope funneling used.} We first enforced strict \emph{AutoRL + manufacturing predictive maintenance + journal only + 2020--2026}. Because fewer than five journal papers explicitly implement full AutoRL pipelines, the scope was widened to peer-reviewed manufacturing PdM papers using RL/DRL with automation-relevant components (adaptive tuning, scalable policy learning, semi-automated optimization), still excluding arXiv and conference-only papers.

\textbf{Practical takeaway (as of March 21, 2026).} Journal evidence supports RL for maintenance decision timing under uncertainty; however, explicit end-to-end AutoRL workflows (automated model selection + automated hyper-parameter optimization + deployment governance) remain limited in manufacturing PdM.

\subsection*{Practitioner implications}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Highest value use-cases are sequential maintenance timing, multi-machine coordination, and cost-availability trade-offs.
    \item DQN and actor-critic families dominate the published industrial PdM RL literature.
    \item Hyper-parameter search spaces are often incompletely reported, reducing reproducibility for plant deployment teams.
    \item A robust near-term deployment pattern is health-state prediction + constrained RL decision layer with guardrails.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Priority research gaps for industrial adoption}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Full AutoRL stacks for manufacturing PdM are still rare.
    \item Search-space disclosure standards are weak across studies.
    \item Cross-plant transferability and policy monitoring/governance are underdeveloped.
\end{itemize}
